Mahatama Gandhi

1869 - 1948

Great contribution in the independenceof the country

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule and in turn to inspire movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.
He was also considered as the father of the country.

The following list is a time line of Mahatama Gandhi

  • 1869 - Birth at Porbandar.
  • 1882 - Marriage with Kasturbai, aged 13.
  • 1893 - Refused to remove his Paghadi; left the court.
  • 1893 - Thrown off the first class carriage of the train at Pietermartizburg Station.
  • 1894 - Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'.
  • 1896 - Commenced writing the 'Green Pamphlet' at Rajkot.
  • 1899 - Established Indian Ambulance Corp during the Boer War
  • 1906 - Advent of Satyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, where people took a pledge with God as their witness, to oppose the Black Act.
  • 1908 - The term 'Satyagraha' adopted based on Maganlal Gandhi's fomulation 'Sadagraha'.
  • 1909 - Wrote 'Hind Swarajya' aboard the ship 'Kildonan Castle'
  • 1919 - Congress boycott the Hunter Commission and set up independent inquiry.
  • 1929 - Purna Swaraj Resolution adopted at Lahore Congress.
  • 1942 - 'Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'
  • 1947 - Indefinite fast against the communal violence in Calcutta.
  • 1948 - Took three bullets on his chest. Godse the assassin.
